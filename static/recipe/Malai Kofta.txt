Ingredients:
For Kofta:

2 cups grated paneer (cottage cheese)
2 cups boiled and mashed potatoes
1/2 tsp garam masala powder
1 tsp cumin powder
1 tsp coriander powder
1/4 tsp turmeric powder
1/2 tsp red chili powder
2 tbsp corn flour
Salt to taste
Oil for deep frying
For Gravy:

2 tbsp oil
1 cinnamon stick
2-3 cloves
2-3 green cardamom
1 bay leaf
1 tsp cumin seeds
1 onion, finely chopped
1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
2-3 green chilies, chopped
1 cup tomato puree
1 tsp coriander powder
1/2 tsp turmeric powder
1 tsp garam masala powder
Salt to taste
1/2 cup fresh cream
1/2 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
Chopped coriander leaves for garnishing
Instructions:

1. In a mixing bowl, add grated paneer, mashed potatoes, cornflour, cumin powder, coriander powder, garam masala powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well and form a dough.
2. Divide the dough into small equal-sized balls and shape them into smooth koftas.
3. Heat oil in a kadhai or deep frying pan on medium heat.
4. Once the oil is hot, add the koftas to the oil and fry till they turn golden brown. Drain the excess oil and keep them aside.
5. For the gravy, heat oil in a pan and add cinnamon stick, cloves, green cardamom, bay leaf, and cumin seeds.
6. Once the spices start to splutter, add finely chopped onion and sauté until it turns translucent.
7. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, and sauté until the raw smell goes away.
8. Add tomato puree, coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder, and salt to the pan. Cook for 5-6 minutes on low heat till the gravy thickens.
9. Add fresh cream and mix well.
10. Add koftas to the gravy and cook for 2-3 minutes on low heat.
11. Add kasuri methi and mix well.
12. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or naan.
