Ingredients:

1 kg chicken, cut into medium-sized pieces
3 cups basmati rice
3 onions, finely sliced
2 tomatoes, finely chopped
1 cup yogurt
1/2 cup mint leaves, finely chopped
1/2 cup coriander leaves, finely chopped
1 tbsp ginger paste
1 tbsp garlic paste
2 tsp red chili powder
2 tsp coriander powder
1 tsp cumin powder
1 tsp garam masala powder
1 tsp turmeric powder
1 tsp biryani masala powder
1/2 tsp nutmeg powder
4-5 green cardamom pods
4-5 cloves
2 bay leaves
1 cinnamon stick
1/2 cup ghee or oil
Salt to taste
Saffron strands soaked in milk for garnish
Instructions:

1. Wash and soak the basmati rice for 30 minutes.
Heat ghee/oil in a heavy-bottomed pan, add the sliced onions and fry until golden brown. Remove half of the onions and keep them aside for later use.
2. To the remaining onions, add ginger and garlic paste and sauté for a minute.
3. Add chopped tomatoes and sauté until they turn soft and mushy.
4. Add red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, turmeric powder, and biryani masala powder to the pan and mix well.
5. Add chicken pieces to the masala and mix well.
Cook for 10 minutes until the chicken is half cooked.
Add yogurt, mint leaves, coriander leaves, nutmeg powder, green cardamom pods, cloves, bay leaves, and cinnamon stick to the pan and mix well.
6. Cook for another 10 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and the masala has thickened.
7. Boil the soaked rice in a large pot of water until it is 3/4th cooked, then drain the water.
8. In a large, heavy-bottomed pan, spread a layer of rice at the bottom. Then add a layer of chicken and masala on top of the rice.
9. Repeat this process until all the rice and chicken is used up. Make sure the top layer is rice.
10. Sprinkle the reserved fried onions on top of the rice.
11. Cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid and place it on a tawa or griddle on low heat.
12. Cook for 25-30 minutes on low heat until the biryani is fully cooked and the rice is fluffy and fragrant.
13.Garnish with saffron strands soaked in milk.
14. Serve hot with raita and salad.
