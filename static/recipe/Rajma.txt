Rajma is a popular vegetarian dish from the Indian subcontinent made with red kidney beans in a thick and spicy gravy. It is often served with steamed rice.

Here's a recipe for Rajma for one serving along with its nutrient information:

Ingredients:

1/4 cup of dry red kidney beans
1 small onion, finely chopped
1 small tomato, finely chopped
1 green chili, finely chopped
1/2 tsp cumin seeds
1/2 tsp turmeric powder
1/2 tsp red chili powder
1/2 tsp coriander powder
1/2 tsp garam masala powder
Salt, to taste
1 tbsp vegetable oil
Coriander leaves, for garnishing
Instructions:

1. Soak the red kidney beans in water for at least 8 hours or overnight. Drain the water and rinse the beans before cooking.
2. In a pressure cooker, add the soaked beans and 2 cups of water. Pressure cook for 3-4 whistles or until the beans are soft and tender.
3. In a pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and allow them to splutter.
4. Add the chopped onion and green chili. Sauté until the onions turn translucent.
5. Add chopped tomatoes and sauté until they turn soft and mushy.
6. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala powder. Mix well.
7. Add the cooked red kidney beans along with the water it was cooked in. Mix well and bring it to a boil.
8. Add salt to taste and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice.
