Ingredients:

500 grams mutton, washed and cleaned
2 cups basmati rice, soaked in water for 30 minutes
2 onions, thinly sliced
2 tomatoes, chopped
1/2 cup yogurt
2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
2 green chillies, chopped
2 teaspoons red chilli powder
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
2 teaspoons coriander powder
1 teaspoon cumin powder
1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
2 bay leaves
4 cloves
4 green cardamom
2 black cardamom
1 cinnamon stick
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
Salt to taste
3 tablespoons ghee
3 tablespoons oil
2 cups hot water
2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves for garnishing

Instructions:

1. Heat the ghee and oil in a large pot. Add the sliced onions and fry until golden brown. Remove half of the onions and keep aside for later use.
2. Add the ginger-garlic paste, chopped tomatoes and green chillies to the remaining onions in the pot. Cook for 5-6 minutes until the tomatoes are soft and mushy.
3. Add the mutton pieces to the pot and cook for 10-15 minutes until the meat is browned on all sides.
4. Add the red chilli powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala powder, bay leaves, cloves, green cardamom, black cardamom, cinnamon stick, cumin seeds and salt to the pot. Mix well.
5. Add the yogurt and mix well. Cook for another 5-7 minutes.
6. Add 2 cups of hot water to the pot. Mix well and bring to a boil.
7. Cover the pot and simmer for 30-40 minutes until the mutton is cooked and tender.
8. While the mutton is cooking, cook the soaked basmati rice in a separate pot until half cooked.
9. Drain the rice and spread it evenly over the cooked mutton in the pot.
10. Sprinkle the reserved fried onions over the rice.
Cover the pot and cook on low heat for another 15-20 minutes until the rice is fully cooked and the flavors are well blended.
11. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot.
