Ingredients:

1/4 cup yellow lentils (toor dal), rinsed and soaked for 30 minutes
1/2 cup water
1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
Salt to taste
1 tablespoon oil or ghee
1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
1 small onion, finely chopped
1 green chili, finely chopped
1/2 inch ginger, finely chopped
2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
1 small tomato, finely chopped
1/4 teaspoon red chili powder
1/4 teaspoon garam masala powder
1 tablespoon chopped coriander leaves
Instructions:

1. In a pressure cooker, add the soaked lentils, water, turmeric powder, and salt. Pressure cook for 2-3 whistles until the lentils are cooked and soft.

2. Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds and let them splutter.

3. Add the chopped onion, green chili, ginger, and garlic. Saute for 2-3 minutes until the onion turns translucent.

4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy.

5. Add red chili powder and garam masala powder. Mix well and cook for a minute.

6. Add the cooked lentils along with the water and mix well. If the dal is too thick, add some more water.

7. Let the dal simmer on low flame for 5-7 minutes.

8. Turn off the flame and add chopped coriander leaves. Mix well.

Your Dal Tadka is now ready to be served hot with steamed rice or roti.
