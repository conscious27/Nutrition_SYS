Ingredients:

200g paneer, grated
2 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
2 tbsp cornflour
Salt, to taste
1/2 tsp red chili powder
1/2 tsp garam masala powder
Oil for frying
1 onion, finely chopped
1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
2 tomatoes, pureed
1 tsp cumin seeds
1 tsp coriander powder
1/2 tsp turmeric powder
1/2 tsp red chili powder
Salt, to taste
1 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
1/4 cup fresh cream
1 tbsp butter
Chopped coriander leaves for garnishing

Instructions:

1. In a bowl, mix grated paneer, mashed potatoes, cornflour, salt, red chili powder, and garam masala powder. Mix everything well until it forms a smooth dough.

2. Divide the dough into small portions and shape them into small balls.

3. Heat oil in a pan and deep fry the paneer balls until golden brown. Remove and keep aside.

4. In the same pan, heat 1 tbsp oil and saute the chopped onions until they turn golden brown.

5. Add ginger-garlic paste and saute for another 2 minutes.

6. Add tomato puree, cumin seeds, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix everything well and let it cook for 10-12 minutes.

7. Crush the kasuri methi between your palms and add it to the gravy. Mix well.

8. Add fresh cream and mix well. Let it cook for 2-3 minutes.

9. Add the fried paneer balls to the gravy and mix well.

10. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot with naan or rice.
